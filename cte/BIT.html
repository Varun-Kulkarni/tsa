Rock Ridge Business & IT Courses

Business and Information Technology courses are one of the four areas of CTE courses at Rock Ridge High School. These courses focus on and stress the economy and the role of the economy in our everyday lives. They also focus on entrepreneurship and the skills needed to manage a business. Students learn important uses of technology in the economy that we live today, which revolves around a lot of technology on its own. In addition to this, students are encouraged in these courses to actively participate in the Future Business Leaders of America (FBLA) organization.

Intro to Business & Marketing

The Intro to Business and Marketing course is a course that introduces marketing concepts to students and falls in the Business & IT and Marketing Education curriculum. One of the main things that this course teaches is how to live in a free market economy. It teaches students how to make adapted decisions based on how the economy is fairing. The course also teaches students about their role in the economy as citizens and consumers, but also about strategies used by producers to keep the community involve in their economy.

Personal Finance

The Personal Finance course is a course designed to teach students about making good financial decisions throughout their lives as well as the challenges that they may face as they move on. This course hones in on key aspects of making financial decisions such as banking, credit, insurance, taxes, investments, and inheritance to name a few. Throughout this course, students develop basic skills for financial literacy and analysis. Students also study the skills needed for entry-level employment in finance-related jobs. This course adopts financial literacy skills and objectives in the Code of Virginia ï¿½22.1-200-03B.

Leadership Development

Leadership Development is a unique course that focuses specifically on the skills of a business leader. Students learn about the importance of cooperation in the workforce and how working together, but also having a clear leader, can help tremendously in future jobs. They learn about principles of being a good leader, such as parliamentary procedure and law, public speaking, human relations, and effective communication. The course also provides opportunities for students to show what they have learned with many community service projects and activities, and it stresses the involvement in business organizations such as FBLA

Personal Finance, Intro to Business, and Leadership are taught by Ms. Janet Dewey

Business Law

In the Business Law course, students learn about the laws established in our country and how these laws came to be. In addition to this, the course goes much more in depth about what it means to be an American citizen Students studying in this course learn economic and social values and knowledge through practical studies and skills. Areas that this course focuses on during the school year include: contracts, consumer protection, criminal law, tort law, international law, family/domestic law, employment law, and careers in the legal profession.

Business Management

Business Management is a course in which students explore management in leadership skills on an industry level basis. The course delves into important skills used in owning a business, such as planning steps for a business, marketing and finances, economical decisions, communications, the global market, and social relations. Concepts, project management, and designing based on ethical values are also heavily focused on in this course. Students in this course can enhance their learning by taking certain internships that are provided by the school and/or join business leadership projects such as the Future Business Leaders of America (FBLA).

Computer Info Systems

Computer Info Systems, or CIS as it is usually referred to, is a course that involves students to develop professional level skills through many different projects throughout the school year. Students use Microsoft Office as a versatile tool to develop advanced presentations, statistical reports, etc. Students apply logic and problem-solving to solve basic problems with word processors, spreadsheets, databases, and other multimedia. They also work in groups to explore networking, operating systems, telecommunications, and other emerging technologies. In addition, students can get a core level Microsoft Office Specialist (MOS) 2010 certification certify that they can use every extent of Microsoft Office.

Advanced Computer Info Systems

Advanced CIS takes student's Microsoft Office skills learned in Computer Info Systems and puts them to the test. Advanced CIS gives students the opportunity to participate in a variety of project and research activities throughout the school year. Students use critical thinking and problem solving combined with advanced software applications to make the most out of their projects. Projects can be print publications, electronic publications, or even website publications. Students learn more about computer maintenance, programming, software development, and database development skills in addition to exploring new technologies and employability skills

All CIS courses and Business Law/Management courses are taught by Mrs. Roxana Cromwell

About Ms. Janet Dewey

Ms. Dewey is a Business Education teacher at RRHS. Prior to joining the Rock Ridge faculty, she taught Personal Finance at Briar Woods HS. Ms. Dewey is a career switcher, having worked for 20 years in financial marketing at several banks and credit unions. An Ohio native, she is a graduate of Kent State University with a B.S. in Advertising. She completed her teacher certification training at Shenandoah University and also holds a Certification in Personal Finance (CPF). E-mail: Janet.Dewey@lcps.org

About Mrs. Roxana Cromwell

Roxana Cromwell is a graduate of Pennsylvania State University with an M.B.A. in Business. Her undergraduate Bachelor of Science degree in Marketing comes from Northeastern University in Boston, where she grew up. Mrs. Cromwell worked for over ten (10) years in private industry for Kraft Foods, Friendly Ice Cream Corporation, MCI and AFLAC Insurance. Her work experience includes product management, product marketing, consumer promotion and business development. Mrs. Cromwell began her career with Loudoun County Public Schools in 2005 as a substitute and obtained her teaching license from Old Dominion University's Career Switcher Program in 2007. Mrs. Cromwell currently teaches Computer Information Systems, Advanced Computer Information Systems, Business Law (S1) and Business Management (S2). In addition to the classes taught, Mrs. Cromwell is one of the FBLA (Future Business Leaders of America) advisers. E-mail: Roxana.Cromwell@lcps.org
