Rock Ridge Marketing Education Courses

Marketing Education courses at Rock Ridge build on and highly stress students' leadership skills, their awareness of civic responsibilities, and their understanding of the job market. The courses teach students how to make good decisions in the economy that we live in today and encourage students to think like entrepreneurs. Additionally, marketing education also urges students to participate in the Distributive Education Clubs of America, more commonly known by its abbreviation, DECA. This organization allows students to participate in activities in local, state, and national levels and is a competitive program that prepares students for challenges in marketing and finance.

Intro to Business & Marketing

The Intro to Business and Marketing course is a course that introduces marketing concepts to students and falls in the Business & IT and Marketing Education curriculum. One of the main things that this course teaches is how to live in a free market economy. It teaches students how to make adapted decisions based on how the economy is fairing. The course also teaches students about their role in the economy as citizens and consumers, but also about strategies used by producers to keep the community involve in their economy.

Sports, Entertainment & Recreation Marketing

This course is designed for students with an interest in sports and entertainment industry. It offers a unique design that encourages students to explore understanding of sports, recreation, and other industries as well as an understanding of skills such as business strategy planning, licensing products, dealing with agents, examining of on-site merchandising, analyzing markets, and executing an event correctly. In addition to this students participate in projects involving DECA.

Marketing Co-Op and Non Co-Op

The Marketing Co-Op and Marketing Non Co-Op courses are two of the most popular marketing courses our school offers to students. In this course, students learn about entry level jobs and the curriculum of this course combines the classroom instructional environment with supervised on-the-job training. Students learn about what is needed in the areas of human relations, communications, advertising, display, operations, sales, and products. The students use computer activities and simulations to learn about how to think logically and critically in a business environment. In addition to this, guest speakers come to the school and talk with the students about various topics regarding economics and the workforce. Co-Op students are expected to be employed for the full year in a job related to marketing that is approved by the teacher. In addition to this, DECA plays a big role in this course.

About Mrs. Christine Good

All Marketing courses this year are taught by Mrs. Christine Good. E-mail: Christine.Good@lcps.org.Mrs. Good is the Marketing Teacher-Coordinator and Department Chair for Career & Technical Education at Rock Ridge. Prior to joining the Phoenix faculty, she taught Business & Information Technology courses at Broad Run High School. Mrs. Good is a proud graduate of Loudoun County Public Schools, having attended Stone Bridge High School and Heritage High School in their inaugural years. Her education continued at the Pamplin College of Business, where she studied marketing and management. Mrs. Good also earned her Master's degree in Career & Technical Education from Virginia Tech. Mrs. Good holds several industry certifications including the National Retail Federation (NRF) Customer Service certification, Internet and Computing Core Certification (IC3), Microsoft Office Specialist (MOS) 2010 core certifications for Word, Excel, PowerPoint and Access, and the W!SE Financial Literacy certification. Mrs. Good is honored to have received the Virginia Business Education Association (VBEA) Outstanding New Business & Information Technology Teacher award in 2012 and the VACTE Outstanding New Career & Technical Teacher award in 2013. She was recognized as a finalist for the 2013 ACTE Region II Outstanding New Career & Technical Teacher award in Pigeon Forge, TN. Mrs. Good is the 2015 recipient of the Virginia DECA Outstanding New Advisor award.
