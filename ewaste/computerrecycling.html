How to Recycle

  • Computers are broken down into several parts, one of them known as cathode ray tube (CRT).
  • The hazardous material in computers is recycled through a patented process, and the CRT’s are recycled through a CRT machine.
  • The monitors are then taken apart into cables, circuit boards, and the most importantly, the monitor tube.
  • The monitor tube contains toxic ingredients and is made of glass that contains lead.
  • Since the tube is sealed by a vacuum, it needs to be cautiously removed when the monitor is being dismantled.
  • The glass present in the technology is crushed and removed by the help of the Negative Pressure CRT Separation machine.
  • The separation machine then breaks the glass into small particles that can be recycled.
  • Circuit boards are also broken, and magnets attract metals that are produced from the crushed pieces
  • Then, the process re-starts
